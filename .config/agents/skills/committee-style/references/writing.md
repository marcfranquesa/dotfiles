# Writing Reviews

Use this reference for prose, documentation, memos, grants, essays, and other writing where clarity, structure, and audience fit matter more than implementation correctness.

## Reviewer Roles

- formal rigor reviewer;
- narrative/flow reviewer;
- technical/content reviewer;
- local paragraph or section editor;
- skeptical reader;
- audience/venue reviewer when the target audience or venue is known;
- holistic editor.

## Coverage

Assign at least one reviewer to the whole piece and at least one reviewer to local sections, paragraphs, or claims when the artifact is long enough to need slice coverage.

Tell reviewers to prioritize claim strength, structure, omissions, audience fit, and confusing transitions before wording polish.

## Synthesis

Lead with claim-level and structure-level issues. Include concrete proposed rewrites for important issues, but do not flood the result with sentence polish unless the user asked for line editing.
