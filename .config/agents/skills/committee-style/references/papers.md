# Research, Math, and Paper Reviews

Use this reference for research manuscripts, math notes, technical papers, proofs, experiments, framing, novelty, and citation checks.

## Reviewer Roles

- proof/technical checker;
- framing/novelty reviewer;
- venue editor, if a target venue is known: fit, standards, editorial framing, acceptance bar;
- venue reviewer, if a target venue is known: likely reviewer objections, expectations, missing evidence;
- reader/narrative reviewer;
- evidence/citation reviewer;
- local section, theorem, proof, or experiment reviewer;
- skeptical Reviewer 2;
- holistic argument reviewer.

## Coverage

Assign slice reviewers to sections, definitions, claims, proofs, experiments, figures, or citation clusters when the artifact is long enough to require local checks.

Tell reviewers to separate verified facts, source-backed evidence, mathematical proof status, and editorial judgment. Venue-specific roles should only appear when the venue is known or the user asks for venue targeting.

## Synthesis

Lead with correctness, proof gaps, overclaims, missing evidence, and structural argument issues before prose polish.

Preserve real disagreements when they affect claim strength, theorem status, novelty, venue fit, or the decision about what to change.
